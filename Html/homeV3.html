About Us

Where the Past Lives
Where It All Begin

Offers exhibits covering RAF operations in World War II through to modern SLAF combat operations, tracing the force's full evolution.

Originally established in 1993 as the Aircraft Preservation and Storage Unit, it was restructured into a formal museum in 2008 under Air Chief Marshal Goonetileke's modernization initiative.

An Aviation Research Reference Library was opened in March 2013, open to researchers and enthusiasts.

A Craft Lab, added in September 2014, specializes in hand-crafted wooden scale models of both current and retired aircraft.

Aircraft Collection

A Legacy Written in Wings

De Havilland Dove

The De Havilland Dove served as one of the earliest aircraft in the Sri Lanka Air Force fleet, playing a vital role in transport and communication missions across the island during the 1950s and 60s.

Hawker Siddeley HS 748

A turboprop transport aircraft that formed the backbone of SLAF's medium-lift capability, the HS 748 served faithfully in both military logistics and VIP transport roles for decades.

Bell 212 Helicopter

The Bell 212 served as a workhorse for the Sri Lanka Air Force during some of its most demanding operational periods, conducting search and rescue, troop transport, and humanitarian missions.

The Knowledge Hub

Our dedicated Aviation Research Reference Library at Ratmalana holds a curated collection of military aviation records, technical manuals, and historical documents — a gateway for researchers, historians, and aviation enthusiasts.

  • ✦ Declassified SLAF operational records
  • ✦ RAF & SLAF technical flight manuals
  • ✦ Academic research support & assistance
  • ✦ Digital archive access for registered members
  • ✦ Open since March 2013 — free entry for researchers

Visiting the SLAF Museum

Located within the historic Ratmalana Air Base, the museum welcomes visitors every day from 9:00AM to 5:00PM, except Mondays and public holidays. Entry is open to all — come and walk among legends.
