THE SRI LANKA AIR FORCE MUSEUM

Welcome to the official portal of the Sri Lanka Air Force Museum, Sri Lanka's only national museum dedicated to the field of aviation as well as the history of the Sri Lanka Air Force (SLAF).


At the museum, our primary focus is to provide unparalleled service to all our visitors towards expanding their knowledge on the field of aviation which covers both the technological aspects as well as the historical aspects.

Important Information

The museum compound is clearly demarcated. Restricted areas of the Ratmalana Air Force Base are clearly marked; all visitors are strongly advised not to trespass into such restricted areas.

Please check with the museum staff regarding authorization and restrictions for photography and video recording within the museum complex.

ABOUT
US

Sri Lanka's only national museum dedicated to the field of aviation and the history of the Sri Lanka Air Force.

Est. 1993
Ratmalana
Aviation Heritage

A visit to this establishment is sure to be a unique experience for aviation enthusiasts of all ages — offering a comprehensive picture of aviation technology and the proud history of the S.L.A.F.

01 Establishment
1993

Aircraft Preservation
& Storage Unit

The museum was first established as a fully fledged formation in 1993 as the Aircraft Preservation and Storage Unit at Air Force Base Ratmalana with the intention of preserving the history of aviation in Sri Lanka in general and the history of S.L.A.F. in particular.

Further, being located within an operational air base gives the museum a novel real life appeal that no other museum in Sri Lanka can offer.

02 Modernisation
2008

Museum
Restructured

The present form of the museum was initiated by Air Chief Marshal WDMRJ Goonetileke, the 12th Commander of the Sri Lanka Air Force, who initiated a special project in 2008 with the intention of modernising the Museum.

Under this project the Aircraft Preservation and Storage Unit was restructured as the Sri Lanka Air Force Museum. New exhibits and facilities have been added to make it a very modern museum with up to date facilities.

03 Knowledge Hub
2013

Aviation Research
Reference Library

A comprehensive Aviation Research Reference Library was opened on 11th March 2013 under the continuous modernising efforts initiated by Air Marshal HD Abeywickrama.

This library provides the opportunity for any enthusiast to conduct research in the aviation field, housing an extensive collection of aviation literature, records and historical documents of immense national value.

04 Craft Lab
2014

Scale Model
Workshop

A fully fledged Craft Lab was integrated to the facility on 19th September 2014 under the patronage of Air Marshal KA Gunathilake.

This Craft Lab is specialised in hand crafting wooden scaled models of aircraft both currently operational and taken out of service — preserving the legacy of each aircraft in stunning handcrafted detail for future generations.

Present Day

A Living Legacy

From the Royal Air Force operations during World War II to the combat operations of the S.L.A.F. in the recent past — the museum depicts the evolution of one of Asia's finest air forces.
